An Intuitive Grasp on Segmentation Metrics

MICCAI Educational Challenge 2022

Semantic segmentation is the task consisting in classifying all the pixels of an image into different predefined classes. It has many usecases, in particular for medical images, where segmentation (implicitely or explicitely) is often a prerequisite to the diagnosis. The segmented structures, either healthy or pathological, have many shapes and sizes. A lot of research is dedicated to developping models for automatically segmenting these. This website has been conceived to provide an intuitive understanding of different segmentation metrics. Two canvas are provided to draw a reference and a simulated prediction. Metrics are computed on the fly and by hovering over them, a quick description is provided for each. To illustrate the benefits and pitfall of the differents metrics, we designed different presets (binary, multi-class, and two real images; one captured with Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) and the other with fundus). For each preset, we also provide different interactive scenarios. However, the tool is thought to provide total freedom to the experimenter. Feel free to twist it for your own usecase!
